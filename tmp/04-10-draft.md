## 描述（中文，200-300字）

这台被昵称为"白雪公主的棺材"的收音机唱机组合，是现代工业设计史上的分水岭。

1956年，Dieter Rams与Hans Gugelot为Braun设计了这款SK-4。它的革命性在于彻底打破了当时音响设备"越大越好、越复杂越高级"的审美惯性：透明的亚克力盖板首次让黑胶唱片的转动成为视觉的一部分，简洁的白色金属外壳与木质底座的组合摒弃了一切装饰性元素，控制面板上的功能布局清晰得像实验室仪器。

这个设计直接奠定了Braun此后四十年的设计语言，也催生了Dieter Rams著名的"十项优良设计原则"——从"好设计是诚实的"到"好设计是尽可能少的设计"。当Jonathan Ive在设计第一代iPod时，他的办公桌上就摆着一台Braun T3收音机（Rams的另一件作品）。从Sony Walkman到iPhone，几乎所有我们认为"理所当然"的极简电子产品美学,都能在这台1956年的唱机里找到源头。

SK-4不仅改变了德国战后"质量优先、美学其次"的产业形象,更用一种近乎冷峻的克制,重新定义了什么叫"日常之美"。它的透明盖板不是炫技，而是让技术变得可理解；它的极简外壳不是省成本，而是对用户智商的尊重。

## 描述（英文，150-250词）

Nicknamed "Snow White's Coffin" for its pristine white casing and transparent lid, the Braun SK-4 Radio-Phonograph marked a seismic shift in consumer electronics design.

In 1956, Dieter Rams and Hans Gugelot broke away from the era's "bigger is better" audio equipment aesthetic. The transparent acrylic lid transformed the spinning vinyl into a visual feature, while the clean white metal housing and wooden base eliminated all decorative excess. Controls were laid out with laboratory-instrument clarity.

This design became the blueprint for Braun's visual language for the next four decades and crystallized into Rams' legendary Ten Principles of Good Design—from "Good design is honest" to "Good design is as little design as possible." When Jonathan Ive designed the first iPod, a Braun T3 radio (another Rams creation) sat on his desk. From the Walkman to the iPhone, nearly every "intuitive" minimalist electronic device traces its DNA back to this 1956 phonograph.

The SK-4 didn't just rehabilitate post-war Germany's "quality over beauty" industrial reputation—it redefined everyday aesthetics through almost austere restraint. Its transparent lid wasn't showmanship but a way to make technology understandable. Its minimalist shell wasn't cost-cutting but respect for the user's intelligence.

## 为什么重要（英文，100-150词）

The SK-4 represents the birth of modern consumer electronics design. Before it, audio equipment mimicked furniture—bulky, ornate, opaque. Rams and Gugelot's radical transparency (literal and conceptual) established principles that shaped the next 70 years of product design.

It pioneered the idea that everyday devices should be honest about their function, unobtrusive in form, and thoughtfully detailed—concepts codified in Rams' Ten Principles. When MoMA acquired one for its permanent collection, it acknowledged the SK-4 not as a radio but as a manifesto.

This design's influence ripples through Apple's DNA, Sony's industrial language, and countless "quiet" products that let users, not objects, be the focus. In an age drowning in design noise, the SK-4 reminds us: less, but better.
